Diversity and Inclusion Workshops

Promoting Inclusivity in Technology (PIT) is a student-led organization whose goal is to create an inclusive environment within the School of Informatics and Computing (SoIC) at Indiana University. We create and delivers workshops to students, Associate Instructors and staff. These workshops discuss the topics of uncovering unconscious bias, micro-aggressions and micro-inequities. Through these workshops we equip participants with the knowledge and tools to help foster an environment of inclusion and understanding.

NCWIT Grant

In the Spring of 2016 the National Center for Women in Technology awarded the PIT Crew a $15,000 Trailblazer Award. There are two main components of the grant.

GET MAD Campaign

We created this campaign which stands for Gender Equity in Technology: Male Advocacy Development. We held a series of events to show the importance for males to become advocates for women in technology. I personally organized a contest for students to share their experiences with gender and technology. We awarded five student winners at a school wide event featuring distinguished professor, Michael Kimmel of Stony Brook University.

Research

The second component of our award will allow us to study male advocates for women and technology in academia. This research is currently underway. While there has been much research about male advocates in the workplace, we are specifically focusing on academia due to the dearth of research on the topic. We will culminate our research by creating best practices and recommendations for being and fostering male advocates in academia.
