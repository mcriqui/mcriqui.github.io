Primary Research

Each team member conducted interviews with key stakeholders invested in this user group. I interviewed Jeremy Hamburgh, founder of Hitchcraft Dating, a dating website for adults with autism. He also coaches and leads workshops teaching these adults social and dating skills. Our key insight from Jeremy led us to our first direction:

First Concept

Based on our research we created an iPad application a facilitator and high functioning adult with autism could use to practice certain social skills. The user would watch a video about a certain social que. Then, the user would try it himself.  The application would record the session allowing the user and facilitator to replay it after the conversation, reflecting and practicing certain skills that may need improvement.

Wrong Path

Using a paper prototype we tested our solution and received mostly positive feedback. However, we realized our design wasn’t the right design for two reasons:

Technology Centered Design

This design is technology rather than human centered. We are asking high functioning adults with autism to practice difficult social skills with an iPad, rather than with another person. We essentially inserted an iPad application into role playing activities that have proven successful in clinical settings. The recording feature added value, but not enough to outweigh the negative aspects of practicing social skills with an iPad.

Back to the Research

With two days left, we decided to switch our direction for the reasons previously mentioned. This was scary but we had solid research and a good connection with our users that we wanted to create a design that added real value. As such, we went back to our research. Requires Facilitator

Our design relied heavily on a facilitator or therapist to watch the recording with the user and give facilitate the reflection. Our design was based on a huge assumption that someone qualified would be able to facilitate or that a user would be in therapy and could use it then. We wanted to create something that wasn’t reliant on a clinical setting because role playing activities within this environment already exist.

One way that people deal with social anxiety disorder is to use a reminder card that gives them a calming message or image.

When the user has an elevated heart rate or level of shaking, it shows the reminder card the user setup.

Affect of our Design

If a user is in a social situation and gets anxiety, thus looks at his watch when it shows the reminder card, the other person in the conversation might think the user is being rude. This brings up considerations of how other stakeholders would be affected by our design.

Reflection and logging mechanism

For this project we changed direction so late in the game. As an organized and time oriented person making this change was extremely nerve wracking. I was able to do so, however, because we spoke to so many users and generated that empathy which made us want to create something meaningful. Additionally, I had complete trust in my team that we could actually come up with something better in such a short time frame.
