Challenge

We were asked to imagine that Indian University was partnering with GE to use analytics within the Predix software to improve life on campus. With hypothetical access to IU’s databases and thousands of fictional sensors, we had to design an integrated system in an area we identified could add value. Our challenge was to

use the Industrial Internet of Things to improve campus life.

Inspiration

We explored many potential use cases and ways to improve student life on campus. We ultimately inspired by an every day occurrence in one of our classes. The room is constantly too hot so we have to prop open the door with a book to allow cool air to enter the room. This temporary fix lead to a rich and complex problem area. We focused our project on

heating and cooling systems.

User Research

With our focus, we interviewed Nathan, the HVAC manager for IU’s School of Informatics and Computing. A 20 minute conversation turned into an hour long session about the complexities of the heating and cooling system. From this research we derived the following key insights:

  • The heating and cooling system is very complex with the East and West side of the building having   different systems.
  • The School of Informatics and Computing has 11 buildings on campus. Nathan has to go to each of these buildings to manually check on the heating and cooling systems, often multiple times a day.
  • When Nathan receives an issue ticket, he has to manually check on the pipes and vents often relying on his years of experience rather than precise measurements.

 

From our research, we made a user journey map of how Nathan currently works without our solution.

Solution

We identified two ways in which the Predix software could help the SOIC facilities team. It is important to note that our system is not intended to replace Nathan or any facilities them members, but to equip them with the proper information to better manage the heating and cooling systems.

Automation

There are a lot of repetitive tasks that can be easily automated. This would allow the facilities team to focus more on maintaining and improving the system and help reduce costs.

Future Strategies

Scale our solution: We focused on the School of Informatics and Computing but want to scale our solution across the buildings on IU’s campus. Each of the buildings have different heating and cooling systems so our solution would be a layer over these existing systems.

 

 

Improved Energy Efficiency: Once we felt we have effectively helped the facilities team with managing and resolving heating and cooling issues, we then want to divert our attention to using the existing systems to improve energy efficiency.

 

 

Updating Legacy Systems: In our research, we noticed that some of the systems today could be used to more effectively heat and cool the building. For example, there are pumps that bring in fresh air to the interior parts of the building and could be adjusted to bring in more air that could cool the building or heat it up.

Reflection

This was my first time doing a systems design project. It was challenging to think about a system at a holistic level. I was also unfamiliar with heating and cooling systems so our user research was essential to understanding the current system and seeing where our design could add value. It was difficult to nail down what exactly we were designing but using tools like user journey maps proved helpful to articulate our design.
